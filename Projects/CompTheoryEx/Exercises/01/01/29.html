Chapter 1: #1.29

  1. Suppose that $A_1$ is regular with pumping length $p\geq 1$. Then, take the string $w=\texttt{0}^p\texttt{1}^p\texttt{2}^p$ in the language. For any decomposition $w=xyz$ such that $|y|>0$ and $|xy| \leq p$, $y$ can only contain $\texttt{0}$'s and hence $xyyz$ cannot be in the language. We obtain a contradiction, meaning $A_1$ cannot be regular.
  2. Suppose that $A_2$ is regular with pumping length $p\geq 1$. Then, take the string $w = \texttt{a}^p\texttt{b}\texttt{a}^p\texttt{b}\texttt{a}^p\texttt{b}$ in the language. For any decomposition $w = xyz$ such that $|y| > 0$ and $|xy| \leq p$, $y$ can only contain $\texttt{a}$'s in the first copy of the word and hence $xyyz$ will not be in the language. We obtain a contradiction, meaning $A_2$ is not regular.
  3. Suppose that $A_3$ is regular with pumping length $p \geq 1$. Then, take the string $w = \texttt{a}^{2^p}$ in the language. For any decomposition $w = xyz$ such that $|y| > 0$ and $|xy| \leq p$, we have $0 < |y| < 2^p$ so $2^p < |xyyz|< 2^{p+1}$ implying $xyyz$ is not in $A_3$. We obtain a contradiction meaning $A_3$ is not regular.
