Preliminaries: #0.1.4

We write the mutliplication explicitly: \begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix}p&q\\r&s\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix}p&p+q\\r&r+s\end{bmatrix}\\ \begin{bmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}p&q\\r&s\end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix}p+r&q+s\\r&s\end{bmatrix}\\ \end{align*} In order for these matrices to be equal, the entries must satisfy the following equations. \begin{align*} p = p + r\\ p+q = q+s\\ r = r\\ r+s =s\\ \end{align*} Which can be reduced to $r = 0$ and $p = s$. Hence, a matrix in $\mathcal{B}$ must have the form $\begin{bmatrix}p&q\\0&p\end{bmatrix}$.
