Preliminaries: #0.3.4

\begin{align*} 37^{100} &\equiv 8^{100} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv \left(8^{28}\right)^{3}\cdot 8^{16} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv 1^{3} \cdot 8^{16} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv \left(8^2\right)^{8} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv 6^{8} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv \left(6^2\right)^{4} &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv 7^4 &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv 2401 &&\pmod{29}\\ &\equiv 23 &&\pmod{29}\\ \end{align*} In the second step, we can use Fermat's little theorem to say $8^{28} \equiv 1 \pmod{29}$ because 29 is prime.
