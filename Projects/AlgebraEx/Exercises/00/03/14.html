Preliminaries: #0.3.14

Because the set $\{1,\dots,n\}$ are the representatives of all the classes in $\Z/n\Z$ and we have shown that they are invertible if and only if they are coprime to $n$. We have the equality in Proposition 4.

For $n=12$ we list the coprime elements: $\{1,5,7,11\}$. Indeed, all these elements are invertible as is shown below. \begin{align*} 1\cdot 1 = 1 &\equiv 1 \pmod{12}\\ 5\cdot 5 = 25 &\equiv 1 \pmod{12}\\ 7\cdot 7 = 49 &\equiv 1 \pmod{12}\\ 11\cdot 11 = 1 &\equiv 1 \pmod{12}\\ \end{align*}
