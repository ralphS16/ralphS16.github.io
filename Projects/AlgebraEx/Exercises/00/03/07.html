Preliminaries: #0.3.7

Since all squares are congruent to either 0 or 1 modulo 4, we have 4 cases. For $a,b \in \Z$, we must get one of these: \begin{align*} a^2+b^2 &\equiv 0+0 = 0 &&\pmod{4}\\ a^2+b^2 &\equiv 0+1 = 1 &&\pmod{4}\\ a^2+b^2 &\equiv 1+0 = 1 &&\pmod{4}\\ a^2+b^2 &\equiv 1+1 = 2 &&\pmod{4}\\ \end{align*} Hence, $a^2+b^2$ is never has a remainder of 3 when divided by 4.
