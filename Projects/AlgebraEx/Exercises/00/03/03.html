Preliminaries: #0.3.3

Since $10 \equiv 1 \pmod{9}$, any power of 10 is also in the residue class of 1. Hence, by using $\bar{a}\bar{b} = \overline{ab}$, we can write the following. $$a = a_n10^n + a_{n-1}10^{n-1} + \cdots + a_110+a_0 \equiv a_n\cdot 1 + a_{n-1} \cdot 1 + \cdots + a_1 \cdot 1 + a_0 \pmod{9}$$
