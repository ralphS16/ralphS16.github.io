Preliminaries: #0.3.11

Let $\bar{a},\bar{b} \in \left(\Z/n\Z\right)^{\times}$, then there exists $\bar{a}^{-1}, \bar{b}^{-1} \in \Z/n\Z$ such that $\bar{a}\bar{a}^{-1} = \bar{b}\bar{b}^{-1} = 1$. Multiplying them together and using associativity and commutativity, we get that $\left(\bar{a}\bar{b}\right)\left(\bar{a}^{-1}\bar{b}^{-1}\right) = 1$ implying that $\bar{a}\bar{b} \in \left(\Z/n\Z\right)^{\times}$.
