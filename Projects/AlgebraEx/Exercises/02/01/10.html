Chapter 2: #2.1.10

  1. First, $H \cap K \neq \emptyset$ because both subgroups contain the identity. Also, for any $x, y \in H\cap K$, we have $x, y^{-1} \in H$ and $x,y^{-1} \in K$, so $xy^{-1} \in H$ and $xy^{-1} \in K$, hence $xy^{-1} \in H \cap K$, and we obtain that $H \cap K$ is a subgroup.
  2. Denote the collection $\{H_{\alpha}\}_{\alpha \in I}$ and its intersection $H$. The set $H$ is not empty because each subgroup in the collection contains the identity. Moreover, with a similar argument as above, we have $$x,y \in H \Leftrightarrow \forall \alpha \in I, x,y^{-1} \in H_{\alpha} \Leftrightarrow \forall \alpha \in I, xy^{-1} \in H_{\alpha} \Rightarrow xy^{-1} \in H,$$ thus $H$ is a subgroup.
