Denote the torsion subgroup by $H$. It is clear that $H \neq \emptyset$ because $1 \in H$. Also, if $x \in H$, then $x^{-1}$ is also in $H$ as we have $|x| = |x^{-1}|$. Finally, if $x,y \in H$, then $|xy| = \text{lcm}(x,y) \leq xy < \infty$, so $H$ is indeed a subgroup of $G$. Note that we use the commutativity of $G$ to conclude that $|xy| = \text{lcm}(x,y)$.

Consider the group $\text{GL}_2(\Q)$, we give two element $g,h$ of order two with $|gh| = \infty$: \[g = \begin{bmatrix}0&1\\1&0 \end{bmatrix}, \quad h = \begin{bmatrix}0&2\\\frac{1}{2}&0 \end{bmatrix}, \quad gh = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{1}{2}&0\\0&2 \end{bmatrix}\]
