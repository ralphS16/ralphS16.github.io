Chapter 2: #2.2.1

Denote the R.H.S. with $C'_G(A)$. It is clear that if $g \in C'_G(A)$, then $g^{-1} \in C_G(A)$, but then because $C_G(A)$ is a subgroup of $G$, also $g \in C_G(A)$. Converesely, if $g \in C_G(A)$, then $g^{-1} \in C_G(A)$, or equivalently $g \in C'_G(A)$. We conclude that $C_G(A) = C'_G(A)$.
