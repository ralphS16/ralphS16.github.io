Chapter 1: #1.1.29

Let $A \times B$ be abelian then for any $a_1,a_2 \in A$ and $b_1, b_2 \in B$, we have the following $$(a_1,b_1)(a_2,b_2) = (a_2,b_2)(a_1,b_1) \Leftrightarrow (a_1a_2, b_1b_2) = (a_2a_1, b_2b_1) \Leftrightarrow a_1a_2 = a_2a_1 \mbox{ and } b_1b_2 = b_2b_1$$ If $A$ and $B$ are abelian, for any $(a_1,b_1), (a_2,b_2) \in A \times B$, we have the following $$(a_1,b_1)(a_2,b_2) = (a_1a_2, b_1b_2) = (a_2a_1, b_2b_1) = (a_2,b_2)(a_1,b_1)$$
