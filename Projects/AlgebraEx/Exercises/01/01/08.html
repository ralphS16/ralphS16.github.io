Chapter 1: #1.1.8

  1. 1 is clearly the identity in this group. Let $x,z \in G$ then for some integers $n$ and $m$, we have $x^n = z^m = 1$. We know that multplication is commutative in $\C$ so $(xz)^{nm} = x^{nm}z^{nm} = (x^n)^m(z^m)^n = 1$ hence $xz \in G$.
  2. $1+1 = 2$ Exponentiation keeps the magintude of the number in $\C$ so $2^n \neq 1$ for any $n$.
