Chapter 1: #1.1.15

We have seen that this formula holds for $n=2$, assume it works for some $n$ then we show it holds for $n+1$. \begin{align*} (a_1a_2\cdots a_{n+1})^{-1} &= \left((a_1\cdots a_n)(a_{n+1})\right)^{-1}\\ &= a_{n+1}^{-1}\left(a_1\cdots a_n\right)^{-1}\\ &= a_{n+1}^{-1}a_n^{-1}\cdots a_2^{-1}a_1^{-1}\\ \end{align*} Induction finishes this proof.
