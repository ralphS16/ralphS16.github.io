Chapter 1: #1.1.19

  1. Expand the exponentiation and count the number of $x$'s.
  2. We can cancel all the $x$'s when we expand. $$x^ax^{-a} = x\cdots x \cdot x^{-1}\cdots x^{-1} = 1$$ The statement then follows from the uniqueness of the inverse.
  3. For the first part, you can still count the numbers in the expansion of $x^{a+b}$. For the second part, use the second part for positive integers and part b.
