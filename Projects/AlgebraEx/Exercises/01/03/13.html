Chapter 1: #1.3.13

($\Leftarrow$) Let $\sigma = \sigma_1\cdots\sigma_k$ be the disjoint cycle decomposition of $\sigma$. If all $\sigma_i$'s are transpositions, $\sigma_i^2 =\text{id}$ for all $i$ and since disjoint cycles commute, $\sigma^2 = \sigma_1^2\cdots\sigma_k^2 = \text{id}$.

($\Rightarrow$) We prove the contrapositive. Suppose $\sigma$ is not a product of 2-cycles, then without loss of generality, $|\sigma_1| > 2$, then $\sigma_1^2 \neq \text{id}$. Since $\sigma_1$ is disjoint with all other $\sigma_i$'s, $\sigma_2^2\cdots\sigma_k^2$ cannot be the inverse of $\sigma_1^2$ and we conclude that $\sigma_1^2 \neq \text{id}$.
