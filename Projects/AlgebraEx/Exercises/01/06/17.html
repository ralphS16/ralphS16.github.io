Chapter 1: #1.6.17

($\Rightarrow$) Suppose $f:g \mapsto g^{-1}$ is a homomorphism and let $x,y\in G$, then we have $$xy = f((xy)^{-1}) = f(y^{-1}x^{-1}) = f(y{-1})f(x^{-1}) = yx,$$ so $G$ is abelian.

($\Leftarrow$) Suppose $G$ is abelian and let $x, y \in G$, then we have $$ f(xy) = (xy)^{-1} = x^{-1}y^{-1} = f(x)f(y),$$ so $f$ is a homomorphism.
