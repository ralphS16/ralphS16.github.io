Chapter 1: #1.6.12

We claim the map $f: G\times C \rightarrow A \times H$ defined by $[(a,b), c] \mapsto [a,(b,c)]$ is an isomorphism. Again, it is clear that $[a,(b,c)] \mapsto [(a,b),c]$ is a two-sided inverse, so $f$ is bijective. Also, we have $$f\left([(a_1,b_1),c_1][(a_2,b_2),c_2]\right) = f\left([(a_1a_2,b_1b_2),c_1c_2]\right) = [a_1a_2,(b_1b_2,c_1c_2)] = [a_1,(b_1,c_1)][a_2,(b_2,c_2)] = f\left([(a_1,b_1),c_1]\right) = f\left([(a_2,b_2),c_2]\right),$$ hence $f$ is a homomorphism.
