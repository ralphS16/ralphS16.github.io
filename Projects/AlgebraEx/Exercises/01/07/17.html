Chapter 1: #1.7.17

Denote the map of conjugation by $\varphi$, then $\varphi(g_1g_2) = \varphi(g_1)\varphi(g_2)$ as shown in 1.6.16, so $\varphi$ is a homomorphism. Suppose that $x \in \ker(\varphi)$, we get the following logical implications: $$ g(xg^{-1}) = 1 \implies xg^{-1} = g^{-1} \implies x = 1.$$ Therefore, the kernel of $\varphi$ is trivial and it is an isomorphism because the cardinality of the domain and image are the same. The deductions are trivial: the first one follows from the fact that the order of $\varphi(x)$ must divide the order of $x$ and vice-versa, the second one follows from the injectiveness of $\varphi$.
