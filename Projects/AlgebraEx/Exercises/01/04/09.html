Chapter 1: #1.4.9

Let $A,B,C \in \R^{2 \times 2}$, we want to show $(AB)C = A(BC)$. Let $f_M:\R^2 \rightarrow \R^2$ be the function represented by a $2 \times 2$ matrix with real entries. We will show that $f_A \circ f_B$ is the function corresponding to $AB$. It follows that multiplication is associative because function composition is associative. $$ A = \begin{bmatrix}a_1& a_2\\a_3& a_4\end{bmatrix} \quad \quad B = \begin{bmatrix}b_1& b_2\\b_3& b_4\end{bmatrix}$$ \begin{align*} (f_A \circ f_B)\vector{x}{y}&= f_A\vector{b_1x+ b_2y}{b_3x+b_4y}\\ &= \vector{a_1(b_1x+ b_2y) + a_2(b_3x+b_4y)}{a_3(b_1x+ b_2y) + a_4(b_3x+b_4y)}\\ &= \begin{bmatrix}a_1b_1+a_2b_3 & a_1b_2 + a_2b_4\\a_3b_1+a_4b_3& a_3b_2 + a_4b_4\end{bmatrix}\\ & = AB\vector{x}{y} \end{align*} This shows $f_A \circ f_B$ corresponds to the matrix $AB$ and finishes the proof.
