Chapter 1: #1.4.8

\(\newcommand\udots{\mathinner{ \kern1mu\raise1pt{.} \kern2mu\raise4pt{.} \kern2mu\raise7pt{\Rule{0pt}{7pt}{0pt}.} \kern1mu }}\)

Define the following matrices: $$ I = \begin{bmatrix}1& & \\ &\ddots &\\ & & 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad \quad D = \begin{bmatrix}& & 1\\ &\udots &\\ 1& & \end{bmatrix} \quad \quad A = \begin{bmatrix}& & 1\\ & &\\ & & \end{bmatrix}$$ Then, we compute $DA$ and $AD$: $$ DA = \begin{bmatrix}& & \\ & &\\ & & 1 \end{bmatrix} \quad \quad AD = \begin{bmatrix}1& & \\ & & \\ & & \end{bmatrix}$$ We can conclude that $(I+A)D \neq D(I+A)$ and it is clear that both $I+A$ and $D$ are invertible, so they are matrices of $\text{GL}_n(F)$ that do not commute.
