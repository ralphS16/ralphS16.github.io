Chapter 1: #1.4.2

We first compute some orders: \begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix} &=\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \begin{bmatrix}1&1\\0&1\end{bmatrix}^2 &=\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\1&1\end{bmatrix}^2 &=\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \begin{bmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{bmatrix}^2 &=\begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \end{align*} For the last two elements, we can infer their order. We know they must have order two, three or six, but suppose one of them had order six, then, $\text{GL}_2(\F_2)$ would be isomorphic to $\Z/6\Z$ and by the order of the elements above, this is not possible. Now, we can check they are not of order two, so they have order three: \begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix}0&1\\1&1\end{bmatrix}^2 &\neq \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \begin{bmatrix}1&1\\1&0\end{bmatrix}^2 &\neq \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\\ \end{align*}
