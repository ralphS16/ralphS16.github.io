Chapter 1: #1.4.10

  1. $$ \begin{bmatrix}a_1& b_1\\0& c_1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}a_2& b_2\\0& c_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}a_1a_2& a_1b_2+b_1c_2\\0& c_1c_2 \end{bmatrix}$$ Since all of $a_1, a_2, c_1, c_2$ are non-zero, the diagonal entries of the product are non-zero, hence the group is closed under multiplication.
  2. We use the simple formula to find inverses of $2\times 2$ matrices: $$ \begin{bmatrix}a & b\\0& c\end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}\frac{c}{ac} & \frac{-b}{ac}\\0& \frac{a}{ac}\end{bmatrix}$$ Since $a,c \neq 0$, all entries are well defined and the diagonal entries are non-zero, so we get that the group is closed under inverses.
  3. It is clear that the identity is in $G$, so $G$ is a subgroup of $\text{GL}_2(\R)$.
  4. Go back to the computations in a. and b. and replace $c$ by $a$ and $c_i$ by $a_i$. We see that for the multiplication and the inverse, the diagonal entries stay equal. The indentity is also in this new set, so it is a subgroup of $\text{GL}_2(\R)$.
